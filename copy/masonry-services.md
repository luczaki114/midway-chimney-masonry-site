### Service: Tuckpointing

**Body:**

Tuckpointing is the most common masonry repair in Chicago — and the most neglected. When mortar joints fail, water gets in. Water freezes. Bricks crack, shift, and eventually fall. Our tuckpointing removes the deteriorated mortar and replaces it with fresh, properly mixed material matched to your brick. Most Chicago brick buildings need tuckpointing every 20–25 years. If your mortar is crumbling or you're seeing cracks along the joints, don't wait — the longer it sits, the more it costs.

### Service: Lintel Replacement

**Body:**

The steel lintel above your window or door carries the brick load above it. When lintels rust and expand, the brick around them cracks and bows. When they fail, the damage cascades fast. Lintel replacement is structural work — not a cosmetic fix. We remove the failed lintel, shore up the opening, install a properly sized steel replacement, and rebuild the brick above it. If you're seeing diagonal cracks at the corners of windows or doors, a lintel is often the cause.

### Service: Parapet Wall Repairs

**Body:**

The parapet wall at your roofline takes more abuse than any other part of your building — exposed on all sides, no roof overhang for protection, and usually the last thing to get maintained. Deteriorated parapet walls let water into the building and, when severely compromised, can become a safety hazard. We repair coping stones, rebuild failed sections, install proper flashing, and waterproof the completed work. This is specialty restoration, and we've done it on buildings across Cook County.

### Service: I-Beam Replacement

**Body:**

In older Chicago buildings — particularly multi-unit residential — structural I-beams carry floor and wall loads. When these beams rust through, the result is wall movement, cracking facades, and in advanced cases, structural failure. I-beam replacement is serious work that requires experience with both masonry and structural steel. We safely remove the compromised beam, provide temporary shoring, install a new engineered steel beam, and restore the surrounding masonry. Not many contractors handle this from start to finish — we do.

### Service: Shelf Angle Replacement

**Body:**

Shelf angles are the steel supports embedded in a building's structure that carry the weight of the brick facade on upper floors. When they rust and fail, the brick above them starts to bow outward — a serious hazard on multi-story buildings. Shelf angle replacement requires removing the brick veneer, replacing the corroded angle, and relaying the brick above it. This is high-stakes structural work. We do it right, with the right materials, so it doesn't need to be done again.

### Service: Waterproofing

**Body:**

Water is the number one enemy of masonry. Once it gets into brick and mortar, freeze-thaw cycles do the rest. Waterproofing creates a breathable barrier that stops water from penetrating while letting moisture inside the wall escape. We apply waterproofing to brick walls, parapet tops, chimney crowns, and concrete surfaces. It's most effective done after tuckpointing or restoration — sealing repaired masonry to extend the life of the work.
